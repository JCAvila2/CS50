What is the World Endurance Championship?

It is a motor racing competition that is disputed between sport prototypes and grand touring cars. It has changed its name and regulations several times, and hosts some of the most important automobile races in the world. It is managed by the Fédération Internationale de l'Automobile (FIA).
Virtually every historic endurance race was part of the championship calendar, including the 24 Hours of Le Mans, the 24 Hours of Daytona, the Carrera Panamericana, the 12 Hours of Sebring, the 1000 km Nürburgring, the 1000 km Monza and the 1000 km Spa-Francorchamps. Numerous sports car manufacturers forged their identity within the World Endurance Championship.
Some of the world's leading automakers compete in this category (or will join in the future), such as:

  • Toyota
  • Alpine
  • Peugeot
  • Porsche
  • Ferrari
  • Cadillac
