What is Motor Racing?

Motor racing is the sport practiced in motorized vehicles, and in most categories consists of completing a course in the shortest possible time.
It is one of the most popular sporting events in the world, and the one that moves the most money, since it involves a large number of companies, manufacturers, athletes, engineers and sponsors.
In this sport, the latest technologies in engines, suspension, aerodynamics and tires are developed to obtain the best possible performance, always keeping in mind to improve the safety of the sport, most of these advances are then used in commercial vehicles, an example of this is the disc brake.
There are different categories, these are the most popular ones:

  • Open-Wheel
  • Sport Prototypes
  • GT (Gran Touring)
  • Rally
  • Drag Racing

Categories

Open Wheel / Single-Seater

In this category are the most famous competitions such as Formula 1, Indycar, Formula E, etc... these vehicles are the fastest around a circuit due to the great aerodynamics they have.
These are the most important single-seater competitions:

  • Formula 1
  • Formula 2
  • Indycar Series
  • Formula E
  • Super Formula
  • Formula 3

Sport Prototypes

These are cars designed exclusively for competition, which comply with the specifications stipulated by the championships in which they compete.
They are usually used in endurance races, which consist of teams with several drivers taking turns in the same car during races of 3, 4, 6, 8, 12 or even 24 hours. They push both man and machine to the limit.
These are the most important prototype competitions:

  • World Endurance Championship (WEC)
  • IMSA SportsCar Championship
  • European Le Mans Series

Grand Touring (GT)

Grand Touring Cars (GT) are modified versions of commercial sports cars, such as the Porsche 911 and its respective racing version, the Porsche 911 GT3 R. Like prototypes, they are generally used in endurance races, which consist of teams with several drivers taking turns in the same car during races of 3, 4, 6, 8, 12 or even 24 hours.
These are the most important grand touring car competitions:

  • GT World Challenge (GTWC)
  • DTM
  • FIA GT3 European Championship
  • British GT Championship
  • Intercontinental GT Challenge

Rally

In this category, riders face the clock to complete a course in different conditions as quickly as possible.
These are the most important rally competitions:

  • World Rally Championship (WRC)
  • Rally Dakar
  • FIA World RallyCross Championship
